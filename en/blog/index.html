Colleague robot

By Focus 11/2015 Page 66 f.

(…) Cologne-based company Cognotekt ñ that automates business processes using artificial intelligence ñ making countless jobs in insurance redundant ñ shows how realistic the risk already is for desk workers. At the moment, the German insurance industry employs tens of thousands of handlers to carry out investigations into whether there is an attempted fraud or whether the insurance might be able to reduce the claim sum. ìIn ten years, no more than 20 percent of these employees will still be neededî, predicts Cognotekt managing director Jobst Landgrebe.

Every claims handler is replaceable

By FAZ on 17.01.2017

(…) The insurance industry is in relentless digital turmoil. Which is almost no difference to other industries. But, it is being hit harder for two reasons: due to the strict regulation of products and companies, there have been market entry barriers, through which technical innovations have long been unable to penetrate the conservative industries. In addition, the linguistic and graphical contents, which insurers deal with, are very complex. As a result, it was more difficult to automate them than in logistics or online trading, where only numbers have to be processed.

Insurers want to be like Amazon

By FAZ on 17.08.2016 Page 23

(…) If Jobst Landgrebe had his way, insurers would have to be much faster. The founder and owner of Cognotekt relies on artificial intelligence as a provider of efficiency. For years, the businessman worked in the insurance industry and recognised that many claims are so similar that they could be automated successfully. He has built a robot for a service provider in the car insurance business, which examines cost estimates or invoices from workshops to see if they are objectionable.

Virtual insurance detectives

By Herbert Frommes Versicherungsmonitor: Dossier on digitalisation of the insurance industry Edition May 2015 Page 18

(…) The first suppliers are already tweaking appropriate technology using artificial intelligence. Cognotekt from Cologne has developed software which is based on a learning, stochastic algorithm. Founder Jobst Landgrebe gives an example: a patient wants to draw a benefit from a sick pay insurance policy. The intelligent computer analyses large quantities of data, for example the medical history, and checks how probable it is that the policyholder is actually ill. For unsuspicious cases, it triggers the payment.
